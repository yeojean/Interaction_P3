DearStranger

Some things are hardest to tell the people closest to us — because of judgment, because the relationship would shift, because the community is too small. Write a letter instead to someone you don't know, chosen by you, and wait for a letter back.

Write the letter you can't send.
Begin a letter Read one waiting
How it works

Three slow steps.

Built unhurried on purpose. Writing a letter is not the same as posting a confession. Both people get time to think.

i.

Write

Open a sheet of stationery. Write about something you cannot easily say out loud. One page is enough.

ii.

Choose a reader

Pick a gender, a life stage, and an experience. Your letter goes to one person, not a feed.

iii.

Wait for a reply

A reader opens when ready. Usually a reply arrives within a day or two.
