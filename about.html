About

Distance, with sincerity.

Dear Stranger is a letter-based peer support platform. It's built on a small, honest idea: that the people we are closest to are often the ones we can say the least to.

Why this exists

People usually can't tell the heaviest things to the people nearest to them for one of three reasons — fear of being judged, fear of changing the relationship, or fear that the story will travel in a small community.

Psychologists call the opposite of this the Stranger on a Train phenomenon: we open up more easily to people we won't see again, because the stakes are different.

Existing tools fail this need. Reddit gives you an audience you can't choose. Whisper gives you a one-off line. 7 Cups matches you to a trained volunteer. Therapy is expensive and often months away.

Dear Stranger sits between these. One letter, one matched reader, one reply.

Ethics & the hard problems

i. Mental health crises

Letters containing crisis signals are routed quietly to a moderator who can reply with resources and a direct line to 988.

ii. Grooming & predatory use

Readers must verify before they can respond. Threads close after a set number of exchanges. There is no private-message upgrade path.

iii. Vicarious trauma for listeners

Listeners have a weekly cap of three letters and a cooldown after heavy replies.

iv. Anonymity vs. accountability

Accounts are verified, but nothing attached to that verification is ever shown to the other participant.

Safety & crisis

Dear Stranger is not a crisis service. If you are thinking about self-harm or in immediate danger, please reach out:

  • 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline — call or text 988 · 24/7
  • Crisis Text Line — text HOME to 741741 · 24/7
  • The Trevor Project — 1-866-488-7386 · 24/7
  • NYC Well — 1-888-NYC-WELL
